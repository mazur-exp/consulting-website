## 1. ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)

### Who they are:

**Primary profile:**
Foreign restaurant owners operating in Bali and Thailand (Southeast Asia), who don't understand local language/mentality and struggle with delivery platforms GoJek and Grab.

**Demographics:**
- Nationality: 95% foreigners (Russian, Ukrainian, European, English-speaking)
- Role: Restaurant owners, General Managers, Cloud/Ghost Kitchen operators
- Age: 28-50 years (typically)
- Language: Russian, English (NOT Indonesian/Thai)

**Business characteristics:**
- Type: Cafes, restaurants, Cloud/Ghost Kitchens, multi-brand operations
- Team size: 5-15 people
- Monthly revenue: $10,000-50,000/month
- Revenue after platform commissions: ~$8,000-38,000/month
- Platforms used: GoJek and Grab (primary delivery platforms in SEA)

**Geographic distribution:**
- Bali: Canggu (40%), Ubud (25%), Seminyak (15%), Uluwatu (10%), Sanur (10%)
- Thailand: Phuket and other tourist areas
- Total addressable market: 300-400 suitable establishments in Bali alone

**Behavioral characteristics:**
- Spend 5-10 hours/week managing delivery
- Struggle with falling ratings (don't know why)
- Don't understand platform algorithms
- Try to figure out Indonesian/Thai interfaces
- Communication barrier with platform support
- Make pricing/promotion decisions without understanding full economics
- Often use discounts that kill margins

**Pain intensity:**
- Current cost: $1,500-3,000/month in owner's time (5-10h/week × $75/hour)
- Revenue impact: Missing 40-200% potential growth
- Stress level: HIGH (constantly worrying about ratings, orders)

**Specific example ICP:**
"Alexey, 35, Russian restaurant owner in Canggu, Bali. Runs a cafe with $25k/month revenue. Team of 8 people. Uses GoJek and Grab for 60% of orders. Spends 8 hours/week trying to manage delivery - adjusting prices, responding to reviews, talking to platform support (language barrier!). Rating dropped from 4.7 to 4.2 → orders decreased 40%. Doesn't understand why. Tried discounts → margins killed. Frustrated and wants to delegate but doesn't know who to trust."

## 2. Value Proposition & Pain Points

### Current workflow / Jobs to be done:

**What clients do NOW (5-10 hours/week):**
1. **Menu management** (2-3h/week):
   - Adjusting prices manually
   - Adding/removing items
   - Writing descriptions (often poor translation)
   - Upload photos (often not optimized)
   - Don't know which items to promote as "heroes"

2. **Promotions & Ads** (2-3h/week):
   - Try to run ads without understanding ROAS
   - Often lose money (ROAS <1)
   - Don't understand what works and what doesn't
   - Communication with GoJek/Grab managers (language barrier)

3. **Ratings management** (1-2h/week):
   - Respond to reviews manually
   - Don't know how to improve rating
   - Try techniques from Google (don't work for GoJek/Grab)
   - Watch rating fall → stress

4. **Problem solving** (2-3h/week):
   - Communication with platform support (Indonesian/Thai)
   - Fixing technical issues
   - Understanding why orders decreased
   - Figuring out algorithm changes

5. **Analytics** (1h/week):
   - Try to analyze data manually
   - Don't understand what metrics matter
   - Can't compare GoJek vs Grab performance

### Pain points:

**Problem #1: Rating falls → orders decrease**
- Typical scenario: 4.7 → 4.2 = 40% fewer orders
- Don't understand WHY (algorithm, reviews, delivery issues?)
- Don't know HOW to fix quickly
- Impact: $3,000-8,000/month lost revenue

**Problem #2: Platform complexity**
- Interfaces in Indonesian/Thai
- Documentation not translated
- Support doesn't speak English well
- Algorithm changes not communicated
- Impact: waste 5-10 hours/week, make wrong decisions

**Problem #3: Discounts kill margins**
- Try to compete with discounts (50%, 30%, buy-1-get-1)

- Result: more orders BUT negative profit!
- Example: 50% discount on hot dogs = -2.3M IDR loss/month
- Impact: work MORE, earn LESS

- Try different things randomly
- No data-driven approach
- Can't measure results properly
- Don't know platform-specific tactics (key priming, rating manipulation, etc.)
- Impact: wasted ad budget, missed growth opportunities

**Problem #5: No time to learn**
- Should focus on kitchen, team, business development
- Delivery management = operational work (not strategic)
- Owner's time = $50-100/hour (too expensive for ops!)
- Impact: $1,500-3,000/month opportunity cost
